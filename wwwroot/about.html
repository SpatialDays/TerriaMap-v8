About CommonSensing

CommonSensing is a website for accessing geospatial information, an easy way to explore, visualise and analyse national and regional data in relation to climate change resilience.

Use the Explore data button to open the catalogue and browse the categories. For specific instructions and more functionalities see Help.

CommonSensing also provides access to the DataCube, allowing users to access products that cover a vast range of use cases, from monitoring vegetation health to analysing coastal erosion through time. For more information on how to use the DataCube functionality, see the DataCube page.

Access further CommonSensing resources at the following links:

  • Met e-Learning website: a course created to support climate officers and those working in sectors that are required to use climate information. Requires separate login details.
  • Decision support system for enhanced risk reduction: a platform to inform decision-makers on disaster risk and its elements.

CommonSensing is an innovative international project based on a partnership between Fiji, Solomon Islands and Vanuatu, and a consortium of international partners, working together to support and build climate resilience and enhance decision making through the use of satellite remote sensing technology.

Discover more about the project at https://www.commonsensing.org.uk/
